Our Commitment

What "safety first"
actually means.

Anyone can put "safety first" in a tagline. Here's what it means at Trailblazers — the specific commitments behind the words.

01 / Personal Protective Equipment

Crew always geared up.

Hard hats, eye protection, hearing protection, chainsaw chaps, climbing harnesses, gloves. The right PPE for the job, every time. Worn correctly, maintained properly, replaced when worn.

If you see one of our crew on your property without proper PPE, tell us. We want to know.

02 / Equipment Standards

Sharp tools, well-kept rigs.

Dull chainsaws cause accidents. Worn climbing rope is a gamble we don't take. Every piece of equipment goes through pre-job inspection — and gets pulled from service if anything looks off.

The investment in good gear isn't optional. It's the cost of doing the job right.

03 / Site Assessment

We walk before we work.

Every job starts with a site walk. Identify hazards, confirm access, plan the drop zones, lock down the work area. We don't pull a saw out of the truck until we know exactly what we're looking at.

This is where the master arborist and 20+ years of combined experience pay you back. We see things less experienced crews miss.

04 / Property Protection

No damage. Period.

Tarps to protect lawns. Plywood under heavy equipment. Slow, controlled rigging instead of free-falling limbs. Whatever it takes to leave the property the way we found it (or better).

If something does go wrong, we own it. We tell you, we make it right, and we don't hide it. That's our accountability core value in action.

05 / Emergency Readiness

Trained for what could go wrong.

First aid kits on every truck. CPR-trained crew members. Clear emergency procedures. Cell coverage planning for remote sites. We come from a first responder background — preparation isn't a checkbox, it's instinct.

The best emergency response is the one you never need. We work to make sure that's how it stays.

06 / Insurance & Documentation

Fully covered. Fully documented.

General liability and workers' compensation insurance — current and verifiable. Proof of insurance available before any work begins, no questions asked. Job documentation maintained for commercial accounts.

If a contractor can't show you their insurance, walk away. We make ours easy to find.

Industry standards we follow.

We don't make up our own rules. We follow the established standards of our industry — and exceed them where we can.

  • ISA (International Society of Arboriculture) standards
  • ANSI Z133 — Arboricultural Operations Safety
  • ANSI A300 — Tree Care Operations Standards
  • OSHA general industry safety standards
  • State of Wisconsin contractor licensing
  • State of Minnesota contractor licensing
  • Manufacturer-specified equipment maintenance
  • Continuous crew training & recertification
Excellence Standard

Quality work.
Clean sites. Happy customers.

Excellence isn't a department. It's how we operate, every day, on every job. Here's what it looks like in practice.

Excellence / 01

The job isn't done until the site is clean.

Branches chipped. Brush hauled. Driveway and walkways blown clear. Lawn checked for stray debris. We leave properties looking like we were never there — except for the work that needed doing.

Excellence / 02

We finish what we start.

No half-done jobs. No "we'll come back next week" for the cleanup. If we said we'd do it, we do it — to completion, before we pack up and leave.

Excellence / 03

We communicate.

You'll know when we're arriving. You'll know if anything changes. You'll know when we're done. No mystery, no chasing us down for status updates. That's accountability and customer focus working together.

Excellence / 04

We stand behind our work.

If something we did isn't right, we make it right. No arguing, no excuses, no "well technically the contract says..." — we own it and we fix it. That's integrity in action.
